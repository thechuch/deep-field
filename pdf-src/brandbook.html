01 · THE IDEA

Depth over noise.

Most wine competes on volume: louder labels, bigger extraction, more adjectives. Deep Fields competes on attention. A few small fields, farmed slowly and read honestly, that hold up under a long stare. The promise, to the buyer and the drinker alike: the more attention you give this wine, the more it gives back.

THE FIELD

A single piece of ground. Soil, roots, place. Lead here.

DEPTH

Of flavor, of roots, of patience. The felt quality in the glass.

THE COSMOS

Earned, never decorated: the field is farmed by the sky.

“The longer you look, the more there is.”

02 · THE MARK

The Deep Fields ring.

One clean circle that reads as an eclipse and a deep-sky aperture at once. Drawn with a fine, confident line, never thick or loud. It is deliberately a single small circle: a quiet nod to the studio that made it.

The mark's meaning is the eclipse and the deep sky: a circle of night and far light. Clear space equals one ring-width on all sides. Never redraw, outline, or fill the mark.

06 · THE FOUNDER

Marne Okafor.

Marne came to wine through the sky. For years she was an astrophotographer, holding long exposures open long enough for faint, far things to arrive. She kept returning to one ridge above the fog because the nights were dark, until the ground under her tripod mattered as much as the sky above it.

So she began to farm it, by the same sky. Biodynamics is farming by the cosmos: the same moon and stars now schedule the work. Long exposures, deep focus, nothing rushed.

“The longer you look, the more there is.”

09 · PACKAGING AND PROOF

Detail is the luxury.

The capsule: ink foil, one gold ring.

The cork: fire-stamped ring, field and site.

The press kit: one page, one bottle, one lens.

Farmed and certified: biodynamic, regenerative organic, and organic. The biodynamic mark wins the natural list, the regenerative mark wins the sustainability-minded chain, and the organic mark reassures the cautious first-timer.

10 · VOICE

Say less, mean it.

SAY THIS

“The fields, farmed by the sky. Nothing rushed and nothing hidden.”

Short sentences. Concrete nouns. Dry warmth. It states the true thing plainly and stops.

NOT THIS

“An award-worthy, ultra-premium, handcrafted luxury wine experience!”

No hype, no exclamation points, no adjectives doing the work the wine should do.
