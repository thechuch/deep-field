THE FIELDS

HALDEN RISE

Deep Fields is grown on Halden Rise, a high, cool ridge on the Northern California coast. It sits above the marine fog by morning and under genuinely dark sky by night. Cool bright days, long cold nights, an unhurried season.

That is the point: slow ripening, retained acidity, length rather than weight. The soils are deep, the vines dry-farmed and deep-rooted, and the whole ridge is farmed field by field, by hand and by the sky.

THE WINEMAKER

MARNE OKAFOR

Marne Okafor came to wine through the sky. For years she was an astrophotographer, pointing an instrument at one dark patch and holding a long exposure open long enough for faint, far things to arrive. It is a practice of patience. The deepest of those images, the ones that held the longest, were called deep fields.

She kept returning to one ridge above the fog because the nights were dark, until the ground under her tripod started to matter as much as the sky above it. So she began to farm it, by the same sky. Biodynamics is farming by the cosmos.

"The longer you look, the more there is."

THE TABLE

PAIRED WITH PATIENCE

Farm-to-glass is not a slogan here. It is the same soil, read twice: once by the vine, once by the kitchen.

FIELD BLEND · SEARED DUCK + MAITAKE

Savory depth meets savory depth. The field blend's dark fruit carries the jus.

Score and slow-render the duck breast skin-down until deeply bronzed, then rest it. Roast the maitake hard in the duck fat. Reduce stock with a spoonful of the wine and a knob of butter. Slice, season, pour, and serve the field blend just below room temperature.

DUSKFALL · SPICED HONEY SQUASH

Skin-contact grip loves sweet-spiced roast. Texture with texture.

Halve a delicata and roast it cut-side down until caramelized. Warm honey with chile and cider vinegar and brush it on generously. Finish with toasted seeds, torn herbs, and flaky salt. The skin-contact grip wants that sweet heat.

AFTERGLOW · OYSTERS + MIGNONETTE

Dusk-pale rosé, cold brine, lemon. The easiest yes of the summer.

Shuck a dozen, keep them cold. For the mignonette: minced shallot, cracked pepper, and good vinegar, given ten minutes to marry. Lemon on the side, the rosé colder than the room. Dusk optional but recommended.
